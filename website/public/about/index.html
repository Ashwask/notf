About Neighbourhoods of the Future

An alliance for local action, treating neighbourhoods as sites of knowhow and collaborative change

The Alliance for Local Action

An unbranded alliance treating neighbourhoods as sites of knowhow and action

Neighbourhoods of the Future (NOTF) is an informal, unbranded alliance that acts as enabling infrastructure. The Nudge Unit brings siloed actors together, lowering the cost of collaboration and making mutual value visible.

Ideas Already Exist

Built on the principle that ideas for local action already exist. Communities know what they need.

Enabling Infrastructure

The Nudge Unit provides the platform, tools, and connections that help actors find one another and work together.

Reducing Friction

Making collaboration easier by connecting Asks (what you need) with Gives (what others can offer).

Example in Action

A community wanted to improve waste segregation. We connected them with a CBO experienced in community engagement, a solution provider offering training materials, and a local government contact. Together, they launched a successful ward-level program.

Our Core Principles

Values-driven collaboration, rooted in trust and local experimentation

Alignment, Not Uniformity

  • Actors don't need to operate the same way to work together
  • The Nudge Unit coordinates around shared intent, not identical methods
  • Diverse actors can move in coordinated ways without being identical

Place-Sensitive Design

  • Solutions evolve from local realities and community aspirations
  • Respond to what people value, struggle with, and hope for
  • Interventions are grounded in neighbourhood context

Participation Over Provision

  • Begin with what people already know, care about, and contribute
  • Shift from 'delivering solutions' to creating space for local participation
  • Ask what people can offer, not just what they need

Adaptable by Design

  • Deliberately loose structure encourages ongoing experimentation
  • Members have visibility into each other's efforts and learnings
  • Enables continuous adaptation and collective learning

Shared Infrastructure

Collating tools, spaces, and expertise to make collaboration easier

NOTF redefines infrastructure to include four key types that enable neighbourhood action:

Physical

Local hubs, community venues, shared tools and equipment

Knowledge

Toolkits, implementation playbooks, case studies, resource banks

Financial

Small grants, pooled funds, shared budgeting resources

Social

Trust networks, citizen groups, CBO connections, peer support

Frequently Asked Questions

Neighbourhoods of the Future is an unbranded, informal alliance that acts as a place-based platform and enabling infrastructure for collaboration. We treat neighbourhoods as the unit of change, bringing together communities, organizations, and local actors to create lasting impact.

It means the Nudge Unit treats neighbourhoods as active ecosystems of knowledge and action. The Nudge Unit aligns different actors' work based on local aspirations and needs, rather than bringing in siloed, outside-in solutions. Every neighbourhood is unique, and solutions must respond to local context.

The alliance is beginning in Bengaluru, Mumbai, Ahmedabad, and Bhubaneswar, with potential to expand. The Nudge Unit is building neighbourhood-level networks in each city, connecting communities and organizations working on climate, liveability, and social equity.

Gives are contributions like domain expertise, programs, physical spaces, CBO connections, or funding. Asks are needs like partners for implementation, tools, venues, small grants, or technical support. The Nudge Unit matches Asks with Gives to enable collaboration.

The Nudge Unit is the first point of contact and the "cartilage" of NOTF. It helps connect members, collate resources, facilitate the matching of Asks and Offers, and provide ongoing support through meetups, help-desks, and a shared proposal bank.
