Ward-Level Tracking

Disaster management targets are tracked at the ward level across all 369 wards to ensure community-level resilience.

369 Wards Monitored

Each ward reports on emergency response plan status, climate risk assessment completion, disaster drills conducted, community training coverage, and early warning system functionality. Ward-level data will be updated quarterly to track progress toward 2026 and 2030 targets.

Data Collection Status: Ward-level hazard mapping underway. First risk assessment reports expected Q2 2026.

Key Actions Being Taken

Initiatives and programs driving progress toward disaster management targets.

Hazard Mapping

Creating detailed flood risk, heat island, and landslide vulnerability maps for every ward.

Early Warning Systems

Installing SMS/app-based alert systems for floods, heatwaves, and severe weather events.

Emergency Shelters

Identifying and equipping schools and community halls as ward-level emergency shelters.

Community Training

Conducting first aid, search and rescue, and disaster response training in every ward.

Ward Committee Empowerment

Strengthening ward committees with resources and authority for disaster preparedness.
